Hiragana is one of the two Japanese writing systems you'll learn first. Unlike Kanji, hiragana is phonetic and all sounds can be read directly.

## The Hiragana Syllabary (Gojuon)

### A-row (あ行)
- **あ** (a)
- **い** (i)
- **う** (u)
- **え** (e)
- **お** (o)

### Ka-row (か行)
- **か** (ka)
- **き** (ki)
- **く** (ku)
- **け** (ke)
- **こ** (ko)

### Sa-row (さ行)
- **さ** (sa)
- **し** (shi)
- **す** (su)
- **せ** (se)
- **そ** (so)

## Tips for Learning Hiragana

1. **Write repeatedly** - Muscle memory helps with recognition and writing
2. **Group by sound patterns** - Learn rows together, not randomly
3. **Use flashcards** - Digital or physical flashcards are very effective
4. **Read simple texts** - Once you know the basics, try reading hiragana-only children's books
5. **Speak aloud** - Connect the visual form with the sound

## Pronunciation Tips

- **Long vowels** - い (i) extends the "i" sound: き → きい (kii)
- **Small ゃ/ゅ/ょ** - These modify sounds: きゃ (kya), きゅ (kyu), きょ (kyo)
- **The ん character** - Represents the "n" sound at the end of syllables

You can learn the complete hiragana set in 2-3 weeks with consistent daily practice!
